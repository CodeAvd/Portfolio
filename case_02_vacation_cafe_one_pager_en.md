# Case 02: Vacation Cafe Simulator Retention Analysis

## Context
The product needed a structured interpretation of “cozy flow” breakdown points and retention risk.

## Problem
Player sentiment was distributed across channels and not directly mapped to actionable product experiments.

## Data Sources
- Steam review/feedback snapshots
- Discord feedback dumps
- Benchmark references from adjacent cozy/management titles
- Internal analysis artifact (`vacation insight.txt`)

## Method
- Consolidated qualitative signals into repeatable friction themes.
- Mapped themes to product metrics (D1, D7, D30, churn, session quality).
- Converted raw observations into design and QoL hypotheses with prioritization logic.

## Insights
- Repetition/friction signal appears around **6–8 hours** for a subset of players.
- Mechanical stress increases near **Level 4–5** when task complexity rises.
- Audio-visual “cozy triggers” + fail-forward mechanics can improve loop resilience.

## Decision
Produced a practical recommendation set focused on:
- Early/mid-loop friction reduction.
- Better progression pacing.
- Higher retention via low-cost QoL and content hooks.

## Impact
- Turned unstructured market/community feedback into a product-ready analysis document.
- Created a backlog-ready hypothesis base for retention and flow optimization work.

## Next Experiment
Run one short-cycle test on a friction-heavy interaction and measure:
- D1 retention delta.
- Quit<10m rate.
- Session completion rate.
