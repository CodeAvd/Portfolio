# Vacation Cafe Simulator — Retention / Friction Analysis

## Public Angle
Player-friction analysis used as a support-adjacent differentiator.

## Context
Community and market feedback described points where the “cozy” game loop started to feel repetitive or mechanically heavy, but those signals were not yet organized into actionable themes.

## What I Did
- Consolidated public-facing feedback from community and review channels.
- Grouped repeat complaints into friction patterns instead of isolated anecdotes.
- Turned those patterns into short-loop hypotheses a team could evaluate quickly.

## Public Proof
- Repeat friction signal around **6-8 hours**
- Complexity spike around **Level 4-5**
- Clear recommendations for QoL and pacing discussions

## Why It Matters for Support Ops
- Demonstrates the ability to turn repeated user pain into something product and support teams can act on
- Improves escalation quality because signals arrive structured, not scattered
- Shows analytical leverage without changing the external identity away from support

## Transferable Value
- Feedback intelligence
- Player-friction analysis
- Customer insight translation
- Support-to-product escalation
