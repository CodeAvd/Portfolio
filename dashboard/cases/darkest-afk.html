Darkest AFK

A support-tooling example built from repetitive player recovery work: faster lookup, clearer package preparation, and more consistent support/admin execution without exposing private workflow detail.

Public takeaway
Less repetitive internal work

Challenge

Player recovery flows required repetitive compensation work. The pain was not strategic ambiguity; it was repeated manual lookup, repeated checks, and repeated formatting decisions inside a support workflow.

Approach

  • Built a static internal tool with search, filtering, and bilingual labels.
  • Focused on repeatable package preparation instead of presentation-only polish.
  • Designed the flow around internal speed and consistency.

What Changed

  • One shared place to find relevant items.
  • Cleaner path from lookup to package preparation.
  • Public proof that support work can produce tooling, not just ticket handling.

Why It Matters Externally

This case signals strong fit for Support Ops and Technical Support roles because it shows workflow standardization, support empathy, and the ability to reduce recurring internal work without waiting for a formal product role.

Transferable Value

  • Support tooling mindset
  • Internal support/admin UX decisions
  • Workflow simplification and standardization
  • Cross-functional coordination between support and admin operations

Confidentiality Note

The public version keeps the workflow story and outcome signals, but removes raw payload structures, internal-only controls, and private operational detail. It is designed to show capability without exposing the underlying system.

Role fit: Support Ops Specialist, Technical Support Specialist, Customer Success in support-heavy environments.
